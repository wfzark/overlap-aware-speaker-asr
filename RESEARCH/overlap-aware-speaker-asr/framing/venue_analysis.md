## Scope of This Analysis

This document compares four candidate venues for the "When Should We Separate?" project — a boundary-aware, compute-aware, speaker-aware ASR routing study with a 21-finding frontier. The project's contribution profile:

- **Core thesis**: Speech separation is not universally beneficial; an overlap-aware router should decide separate-vs-mixed per input.
- **Stable baseline**: 5 gold cases, router v1/v2, CER evaluation, speaker-aware CER, cpCER-lite.
- **Frontier findings**: separation tax (#11), confident attractor / causal hallucination (#21), emotion-ASR asymmetry (#14), objective-aware decoupled routing (#18), speaker-conditioned gate (#13), noise-robust gate (#11).
- **Known gaps**: no external benchmark (M1), oracle separation only (M2), thin statistical power (M3), Whisper-only (E1).

The analysis reverse-engineers each venue's reviewer reward function and identifies the evidence gaps (from RQ1–RQ6) required to meet the bar.

## Recommendation

### Primary: Interspeech 2026 (Regular or Long Paper)

**Justification:**

1. **Best scope and thematic match.** Interspeech is the premier speech-specific conference, and the 2026 theme "Speaking Together" directly celebrates overlapping/multi-speaker speech. The project's core question — "when should we separate?" — is a perfect thematic fit. No other venue offers this alignment.

2. **Double-blind review raises credibility.** The double-blind process ensures the routing thesis is evaluated on merit, not author reputation. This is important for a project with unconventional findings (separation is not always beneficial; emotion has no separation tax).

3. **Page flexibility.** The long-paper track (8+2 pages) accommodates the multi-finding story (routing + separation tax + confident attractor + objective-aware decoupling), while the regular track (4+2 pages) forces a focused contribution. The project can choose based on evidence completeness.

4. **Competitive but achievable acceptance.** At ~48% acceptance, Interspeech is selective but not prohibitive. The project's novelty (overlap-aware routing is a new framing) and timeliness (Whisper hallucination is a hot topic) are strong assets.

5. **Fast turnaround for feedback.** The ~3-month review cycle (March submission → June notification) provides rapid feedback, enabling a journal extension (TASLP) afterward.

6. **Conference presentation builds community.** Presenting at Interspeech connects the project with the speech-separation and meeting-ASR community, generating citations and collaboration.

**Recommended submission format:** Regular paper (4+2 pages) focusing on the routing thesis + separation tax + confident attractor, with the emotion/decoupling findings as a teaser for a follow-up journal paper. This keeps the story tight and the evidence bar achievable.

### Backup: ICASSP 2027 (4+1 pages)

**Justification:**

1. **Tighter, more focused contribution.** If the Interspeech submission is rejected or if the team prefers a more focused paper, ICASSP's 4+1-page format forces a single clear contribution. The confident-attractor finding (#21) alone — a confident, two-mode, causally-early-detectable hallucination phenomenon inside Whisper — is a strong ICASSP paper.

2. **Single-blind reduces anonymization burden.** No need to anonymize self-citations or hide the project's prior findings.

3. **Slightly higher acceptance rate** (~40–45%) than Interspeech, though the gap is narrowing.

4. **Signal-processing community reach.** ICASSP reaches a broader signal-processing audience beyond speech, which may benefit the compute-aware cascade and POMDP directions.

**Risk:** The 4-page limit is very tight for the routing thesis + evaluation. The team must cut aggressively.

### Journal Extension: IEEE TASLP (after conference acceptance)

**Justification:**

1. **Full 21-finding story.** TASLP's no-page-limit format accommodates the complete contribution: routing, separation tax, emotion asymmetry, objective-aware decoupling, confident attractor, gates, and the POMDP framework.

2. **Highest prestige.** JIF 4.1, Q1 Acoustics, CAAI-A. A TASLP paper is the strongest publication outcome for this project.

3. **Journal extension path.** A common pattern: submit the core routing + separation-tax finding to Interspeech/ICASSP, then extend with the emotion/decoupling/hallucination findings + external benchmark + theoretical framework for TASLP. IEEE explicitly permits journal extensions of conference papers with ≥30% new content.

4. **High evidence bar.** TASLP requires external benchmark (RQ1), realistic separator (RQ2), statistical robustness (RQ3), and cross-model validation (RQ4). These are the same gaps identified in `gap_analysis.md`. The journal submission should be timed after RQ1–RQ4 are addressed.

### Not Recommended as Primary: Speech Communication

**Rationale:** Speech Communication is a solid journal (JIF 3.0, Q2 Acoustics) but lower prestige than TASLP and slower/higher-friction than a conference. It is best used as a secondary outlet for the emotion-ASR asymmetry mechanism (RQ6) if that finding is developed into an interdisciplinary speech-science paper. The routing thesis is better served by a conference (faster feedback, higher visibility) or TASLP (higher prestige).

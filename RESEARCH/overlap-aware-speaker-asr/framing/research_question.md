## Main Research Questions

### RQ1 (Confirmatory, PICO) — External benchmark generalization

**Main RQ**: Does the overlap-aware router v2, trained on 5 gold + synthetic silver cases, generalize to a standard meeting benchmark (AISHELL-4) under cpWER evaluation?

**PICO breakdown:**
- **P**opulation: Overlapping speech from AISHELL-4 meeting recordings (multi-speaker, Chinese)
- **I**ntervention: Router v2 routing policy (separate-vs-mixed by overlap + compression-ratio)
- **C**omparison: Always-mixed, always-separated, oracle-best
- **O**utcome: cpWER (MeetEval), CER, routing accuracy vs. oracle

**Sub-questions:**
1. Does the LightOverlap/MidOverlap mixed-better result replicate on real meeting overlap?
2. Does the router's routing accuracy exceed always-mixed by a statistically significant margin?
3. Does the confident-attractor (#21) manifest on real-separator output?

**FINER Assessment:**
| Criterion | Assessment | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Feasible | ✓ | AISHELL-4 is open, MeetEval compatibility already scaffolded |
| Interesting | ✓ | Converts 5-case claim to benchmark-validated claim |
| Novel | ✓ | No prior overlap-aware routing study validates on AISHELL-4 with cpWER |
| Ethical | ✓ | Open dataset, no human subjects |
| Relevant | ✓ | Highest-impact move for reviewer credibility |

**Inclusion Criteria:** AISHELL-4 subset ≤30 min; cpWER via MeetEval; router v2 unchanged
**Exclusion Criteria:** AISHELL-1 (read speech, no overlap); LibriCSS (English, different language)
**Scope boundary:** Does NOT claim SOTA on AISHELL-4; claims *routing-policy transfer* only.

# The Separation Tax — Findings

**Label:** `experimental/frontier` (ASR = Whisper-`tiny`, offline). References are
`synthetic/silver` (Whisper-`small` transcripts of the clean source snippets).
**Stable tables are untouched**; all outputs live under `results/frontier/separation_tax/`.
CER is used for post-hoc evaluation only and is never a routing input.

Generated by `python -m src.separation_tax_phase --pairs 20` (15-point overlap grid,
20 deterministic con×pro speaker pairings, 600 mixture conditions × {greedy, fallback}
decoders + a silence-trim arm) and `--gold-real`. Pre-registration (RQs / hypotheses) is
in the module docstring.

## What question this answers

The stable baseline reports, from 5 gold cases plus a noisy single-sample-per-ratio
synthetic scatter, that speech separation *helps* Whisper at no/heavy/opposite overlap
but *hurts* at light/mid overlap "due to insertion + repetition hallucination." This study
replaces that scatter with a controlled, multi-seed, **oracle-separation** sweep (the
additive mixture's own source tracks = a perfect-separation upper bound) and asks *how*
and *why*, with confidence intervals and a falsifiable cure test.

## RQ1 — The phase curve reproduces the headline finding, continuously

Under the baseline's own decoder setting (`temperature=0.0`, greedy), the mean separation
gain ΔCER = CER(mixed) − CER(separated) is **negative below overlap ≈ 0.17 and positive
above it** (interpolated mean crossover **r\* = 0.173**, median crossover 0.20). So the
categorical "separation hurts at light overlap, helps at higher overlap" claim survives as
a continuous law — separation is a net loss only in a narrow low-overlap band.

| overlap r | mean ΔCER | median ΔCER | mean CER_sep | median CER_sep | sep-tail rate |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.00 | −0.341 | −0.087 | 0.758 | 0.480 | 0.05 |
| 0.05 | −0.579 | −0.033 | 1.003 | 0.440 | 0.05 |
| 0.10 | **−0.943** | **0.000** | **1.401** | 0.510 | 0.10 |
| 0.15 | −0.597 | 0.000 | 1.047 | 0.477 | 0.10 |
| 0.20 | +0.698 | 0.000 | 0.462 | 0.440 | 0.00 |
| 0.50 | +0.110 | 0.052 | 0.458 | 0.500 | 0.00 |
| 0.90 | +0.290 | 0.265 | 0.464 | 0.494 | 0.00 |

## RQ2 — The low-overlap penalty is a heavy hallucination tail, not uniform degradation

This is the substantive correction to the baseline narrative. At overlap 0.10 the **mean**
ΔCER is −0.94 while the **median** ΔCER is **exactly 0.00**, and mean CER_sep (1.40) is
nearly 3× the median (0.51). The "separation hurts" signal is produced almost entirely by
a minority (5–10%) of clips whose oracle-separated track — left with a long silent region
where the other talker was removed — drives Whisper into runaway repetition. For the
*typical* clip, low-overlap separation is neutral.

Concrete receipts (all 6 catastrophic separated tracks in the 600-condition sweep):

| con | pro | r | track | CER | CER (trimmed) | compression | repetition | lead-silence (s) |
|---|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| con_006 | pro_006 | 0.05 | spk2 | 24.25 | 0.50 | 16.3 | 27 | 2.06 |
| con_006 | pro_006 | 0.10 | spk2 | 24.25 | 0.50 | 16.3 | 27 | 1.96 |
| con_006 | pro_006 | 0.15 | spk2 | 24.25 | 0.50 | 16.3 | 27 | 1.86 |
| con_001 | pro_003 | 0.10 | spk2 | 14.87 | 0.60 | 29.1 | 268 | 3.53 |
| con_001 | pro_003 | 0.00 | spk2 | 11.60 | 0.60 | 15.8 | 41 | 3.78 |
| con_003 | pro_002 | 0.15 | spk2 | 1.70 | 0.60 | 1.0 | 1 | 1.70 |

Every catastrophic case is the *leading-silence* speaker at *low* overlap, with an
abnormal compression ratio and repetition count — textbook Whisper silence-hallucination.
Symmetrically, at mid/high overlap the tail flips to the **mixed** input (mean CER_mixed
reaches 1.16–1.22 at r = 0.2–0.4 with non-zero mixed-tail rate), which is what makes
separation look strongly beneficial on average there. The whole "when to separate"
question is, at both extremes, a question of *which input induces a Whisper hallucination
tail*.

## RQ3 — The cure is silence-trimming, not temperature fallback (bold claim, refined)

The baseline pipeline forces `temperature=0.0`, disabling Whisper's temperature-fallback.
I hypothesized that re-enabling the fallback would cure the tail. **It did not** — the
crossover moved the *wrong* way (0.173 → 0.330) and the overall separated-tail rate rose
(0.02 → 0.07). Generic decoding hygiene is not the fix here.

A **mechanism-targeted** cure works decisively: trimming the silent region from the
separated tracks before ASR drops mean CER_sep from **0.618 → 0.440** and collapses the
catastrophic-tail rate from **0.02 → 0.00**. All 6 catastrophic tracks fall from a mean
CER of 16.8 to 0.55 once their injected silence is removed. The separation tax's
catastrophic component is therefore a *silence-induced* hallucination, removable cheaply
and reference-free — separation per se is not the problem.

## RQ4 — Catastrophic tracks are perfectly flaggable without references

A purely reference-free signal — Whisper's segment compression ratio (or the n-gram
repetition count) — ranks the 6 catastrophic tracks above all 594 others (**AUC = 1.0**
for both). A router can detect and route around (or trim) these cases using no ground
truth, consistent with the charter's rule that CER/reference must never be a routing input.

## RQ5 — A reference-free "trim-and-guard" router closes ~76% of the oracle gap

Turning the diagnosis into a deployable policy (greedy decoding, evaluated over all 300
greedy conditions; the guard threshold is Whisper's own default compression ratio of 2.4,
chosen on principle and *not* tuned on CER, so no reference leaks into routing):

| policy | mean CER | regret vs oracle | reference-free |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| fixed_mixed | 0.697 | 0.319 | yes |
| fixed_sep (baseline) | 0.618 | 0.239 | yes |
| always_trim | 0.440 | 0.061 | yes |
| **guard_to_mixed** | **0.437** | **0.058** | yes |
| guard_retry_trim | 0.464 | 0.086 | yes |
| oracle | 0.379 | 0.000 | no |

The best deployable policy — silence-trim the separated tracks, and on the rare condition
where a track still trips the degeneracy guard, fall back to mixed — reaches mean CER
0.437 against an oracle of 0.379, i.e. **regret 0.058**, a 76% reduction from the
fixed-separated baseline's regret of 0.239 and a 0.18-absolute (29% relative) CER cut over
fixed-separated. The guard fires on only 1.7% of conditions (the catastrophic tail). Most
of the gain is the trim itself; the guard is cheap insurance for the tail. None of this
uses references.

## RQ6 — Does it transfer to a real separator? Guard-gated trim yes, blanket trim no

The phase study used oracle separation, so I replayed the cure on the **5 verified gold
cases using the real separator's output** (`resources/separated_audio`, real verified
references, Whisper-`tiny`; these CERs are relative-only, not comparable to the
whisper-small gold table, which is untouched).

| gold case | CER sep | CER always-trim | CER guard-gated | max compression | guard fires |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| NoOverlap | 0.982 | 0.824 | 0.824 | 10.6 | yes |
| LightOverlap | 0.857 | 1.161 | 0.857 | 1.57 | no |
| MidOverlap | 0.863 | 1.102 | 0.863 | 1.62 | no |
| HeavyOverlap | 1.230 | 0.865 | 0.865 | 8.27 | yes |
| OppositeOverlap | 0.855 | 0.866 | 0.855 | 1.49 | no |
| **mean** | **0.957** | **0.963** | **0.853** | | 2/5 |

Real separated tracks are only ~5% silence (not the long silent tails oracle tracks have),
so **blanket trimming does not transfer** — it is neutral-to-harmful on average (0.957 →
0.963) because it can clip real speech on the non-degenerate majority. But the
**reference-free guard transfers cleanly**: it fires on exactly the two cases where
Whisper genuinely went degenerate (compression ratio 10.6 and 8.3), and on those trimming
rescues CER substantially. Applying trim *only when the guard fires* (guard-gated trim)
improves mean CER from 0.957 → **0.853** (−0.105, ~11% relative), using no references. The
lesson sharpened by real separation: intervene on the detected hallucination tail, do not
trim indiscriminately.

## What this changes for the project

The deployable lesson is not "don't separate at low overlap" (the median clip is fine) but
"**separate, then guard-gate a silence-trim / mixed-fallback on the rare hallucination
tail detected reference-free**." On oracle separation this closes three-quarters of the
gap to an oracle selector; on the real-separator gold cases the guard-gated form cuts mean
CER ~11% over fixed-separated — all without ground truth. Natural next falsifiable steps:
(1) confirm it holds for Whisper `base`/`small` (needs model download — blocked offline
here); (2) wire the guard-gated trim/fallback stage into the existing `adaptive_router_v2`
and re-score the held-out synthetic split; (3) test on a real multi-talker corpus
(AISHELL-4 / AliMeeting slice) once licensing/audio are staged.

## Honest limitations

ASR is Whisper-`tiny` (the only model cached offline), so absolute CER is inflated and the
silver references (Whisper-`small` on clean clips) put an effective ~0.4 floor on "correct"
— only *relative* comparisons and the CER≫1 tail are interpreted. Oracle separation is an
upper bound; RQ6 partially addresses this on real-separator output but on only 5 cases. The
corpus is one small Chinese-debate set (11 con / 15 pro, ~3–4 s clips); only 6 catastrophic
positives exist in the sweep, so AUC = 1.0 is encouraging but not tightly estimated.
Concatenation CER with known speaker assignment is used, not permutation cpWER. This is
frontier evidence, not a gold result.
